**Are there non-finite forms that can relativize all core participants (S/A/P) and possibly also some non-core participants?**

A participle may be used to relativize only one specific participant of a situation (i.e., only S/A, P or S/P; see UT055), but there are also participles that can relativize all core participants and possibly also some non-core participants, such as locatives and instrumentals (Shagal 2017: 1-3). For example, Hill Mari *-šašlə̑k* (1) can be used in a wide variety of contexts including for relativizing locatives (Shagal 2018).

(1) Hill Mari<br/>
>*[Vas’a(-n) ə̈lə̈-šäšlə̑k] pört-ə̈m mə̈n’ stroj-en-äm*<br/>
>Vasya(-GEN) live-PTCP.DEB house-ACC 1SG[NOM] build-PRF-1SG<br/>
>‘I built the house in which Vasya will live’<br/>
(Shagal 2018:57)

In some languages, the relevant forms may be referred to as nominalizations or converbs. 

**Coding.** The value is '1' if a language contains at least one participle, which can relativize all core participants and possibly also some non-core participants.
