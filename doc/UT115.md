**Is there a distinction for both 'warm' and 'cold' depending on whether reference is to weather or the temperature of objects?**

This question asks whether different words are used for ‘warm’ and ‘cold’ depending on whether one is referring to the weather or to the temperature of objects. Such a distinction is found, for instance, in Pite Saami.

Pite Saami<br/>
*báhkas : tjåskes*<br/>
'hot weather' : 'cold weather'

*bivval : galbma*<br/>
'hot' : 'cold'

**Coding.** The answer is ‘1’ if such a distinction is made for both concepts. The answer is ‘0’ if there is no such distinction, or if the distinction is made for only one of the concepts.
