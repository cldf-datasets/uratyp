**Can verbs in purposive subordinate clauses be infinitive forms?**

A subordinate (or dependent) clause functions as a constituent of the main clause. Purposive clauses are a type of subordinate clauses used to express meanings like ‘in order to’, ‘so that’ etc. (2). Purposive clauses function as adjuncts - typically of adverbial clauses - as they are not obligatory constituents of the clause. Here, we also considered purposive clauses introduced by a complementizer, such as *et* in example (1b). (The purposive clause may also be called a *final clause*.)

(1) Estonian<br/>
>a. *Ma lähen Ungarisse ungari keelt **õppima***<br/>
>Ma lähe-n Ungari-sse ungari keel-t õppi-ma<br/>
>1SG go-1SG Hungary-ILL Hungarian language-PRT study-INF2<br/>

>b. *Ma lähen Ungarisse, et ungari keelt **õppida***<br/>
>Ma lähe-n Ungari-sse, et ungari keel-t õppi-da<br/>
>1SG go-1SG Hungary-ILL in_order Hungarian language-PRT study-INF1<br/>
>'I’m going to Hungary in order to study Hungarian'<br/>

**Coding.** The value is '1' if it is possible to use an infinitive form in the purposive subordinate clause (1a-b).
