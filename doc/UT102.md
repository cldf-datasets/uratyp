**Can the possessor in the attributive construction be encoded with the nominative?**

There are several ways to encode attributive possession. A marker may appear on the possessor and/or on the possessee, or the two elements may simply be juxtaposed. This question examines whether the possessor can occur in the nominative case, as in Hungarian (1) and Udmurt (2). In example (1), the possessee also carries a 3rd person possessive suffix, whereas example (2) illustrates simple juxtaposition.

(1) Hungarian<br/>
>***Kornél**  kalapja*<br/>
>Kornél  kalap-ja<br/>
>Kornél.NOM.SG  hat-POSS.3SG<br/>
>‘Kornél’s hat’<br/>
>(Rounds 2001)

(2) Udmurt<br/> 
>***val** jir*<br/> 
>horse.NOM.SG head.NOM.SG<br/> 
>’horse’s head’<br/> 
>(Edygarova 2009)

**Coding.** The value is ‘1’ if the possessor in an attributive construction can occur in the nominative case when expressing a central function of possession (e.g., physical possession, body-part or kinship relations). The value is ‘0’ if the nominative case is not used in this function or it only appears in limited or lexicalized instances.
